### August 27, 2019
Lately I've seen headlines like "The earth's lungs are burning" in response to the burning of the Amazon rainforest, citing for instance that plants in the Amazon account for one-fifth of oxygen produced by plants. A friend sent an article today explaining that while the burning of the Amazon is obviously atrocious, it won't result in any noticeable decrease in the amount of oxygen in the atmosphere, and that it really isn't accurate to call the Amazon the earth's lungs. If the image of burning lungs scares people into doing something about these fires, I guess that wouldn't be so bad, but I still feel like the world would be a better place if people didn't flock to big, catchy, and often inaccurate slogans like these.

### August 28, 2019
I met up with a friend from high school today who had a lot to say about how AR/VR, self-driving cars, and drones will shape our future society. While I found myself disagreeing with much of it (I really can't see drones flying around making deliveries to people walking on the street anytime soon, or an self-driving cars eliminating the need for personal vehicle ownership), I was definitely reminded of how short my life has been, how much the world has changed in the years since I started remembering, and how much the world can change while I'm still around.
